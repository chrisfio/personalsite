About me, Chris Fiorino

I am looking to join a talented engineering team where I can contribute to interesting projects and learn from those around me.

I am currently a graduate student studying Computer Science at Georgia Institute of Technology through their OMSCS program. Admittedly, my background doesn’t fit the mold of a typical software engineer, but I believe my work and class experience will help make me a great addition to an engineering team.

My career started in finance after graduating in May 2010, first in the insurance industry before landing a position at an investment bank. While working in finance helped me learn how to work in a high pressure environment, it seemed to me that the industry was outdated and slow to adapt to technology. This observation lead me to start programming in 2012 through Codecademy.

I’ve always enjoyed working with computers, in high school I built and upgraded my own computer, and learning how to code helped renew my interest in computers and technology. Soon enough, I was on the search for a role in the tech sector, eventually finding a job in digital advertising. This role was client facing and analytical in nature but it exposed me to HTML, JavaScript, SQL and Java. Because of this exposure, I knew that I wanted to work with programming languages everyday but knew I needed to learn more before I could make the transition to be a full time software engineer.

In 2015, I started taking undergraduate Computer Science classes in the evenings through local universities and also through online courses at Udacity and Coursera, with the intention of satisfying the prerequisites required to gain admission into a graduate Computer Science program. A year ago, my girlfriend and I decided we wanted to move somewhere new as we have both lived in the Baltimore area for most of our lives. We decided on Silicon Valley, it seemed like the perfect fit for our personalities and a great place for us to embark on the next chapter of our careers. Over the next year, I turned my house into a profitable Airbnb (read more), gained acceptance into Georgia Tech’s OMSCS program and planned for the trip out west.

In September of this year, we arrived to Sunnyvale, CA after a cross country journey in our Honda Civic packed with all our belongings and our cat Turing. I am excited to get the chance to prove myself to a new company. If you’d like to read more about me, click the link to the right to read my cover letter.
